Projects

For the 2020 Scholastic Analytics Challenge, hosted by the Association for Information Systems (AIS), my team analyzed 2,000,000 children's book orders to (1) find demographic trends in sales and (2) create a new strategy to help Scholastic reach all children, regardless of demographic. We used Python to clean the data of duplicate book titles that were inconsistently spelled, and Tableau to visualize the data. Out of 60 schools in the challenge, our presentation won 1st place in the Analysis category. You can read our infographic here.

A favorite project from my Machine Learning class was programming two movie recommenders. The first implements a content-based system: the user selects a movie, and is then returned similar movies based on shared tags (e.g., Toy Story and Shrek might share 'fantasy'). The second implements a collaborative-filtering system: User A rates several films of their choosing, is matched with a User B with the most similar rating behavior, and is then returned films highly-rated by B that A did not rate. You can watch a demonstration here.

Two fellow classmates and I are currently developing our Senior Design project: NiCoMi Analytics. Our mission statement:


Intended for academic use, NiCoMi Analytics will collect data from supported marketing services to generate dashboards that measure the success of clients' online marketing campaigns. These services range from social media to website trackers to ad clients. We plan for a customizable experience, with users being able to choose the metrics included in their dashboards and stylize as needed. Ultimately, NiCoMi Analytics will allow Digital Marketing students to grow in their consultation skills, and local business in their online presence.
